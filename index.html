Madam C.J. Walker

The first female self-made millionaire in America

Madam C.J. Walker driving in 1911

A brief timeline of Madam C.J. Walker's life:

  • 1867: Born in Delta, Louisiana
  • 1874: Becomes orphaned at the age of 7
  • 1877: Moves to Vicksburg, Mississippi, at the age of 10, where she lives with Louvenia and her brother-in-law, Jesse Powell
  • 1882: Marries Moses McWilliams at the age of 14 to escape abuse from her brother-in-law
  • 1885: Has her first child, Lelia Walker
  • 1888: Madam C. J. Walker and her daughter move to St. Louis, where she works as a laundress
  • 1904: Becomes a commission agent selling products for Annie Malone, an African-American hair-care entrepreneur
  • 1905: Moves to Denver, Colorado, where she continues to sell products for Malone and develops her own hair-care business
  • 1906: Marries Charles Joseph Walker, a newspaper advertising salesman, and becomes known as Madam C.J. Walker
  • 1907: Walker and her husband relocate to Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, where they open a beauty parlor and establish Lelia College to train "hair culturists"
  • 1910: Establishes new base in Indianapolis
  • 1913: Sets up an office and beauty salon in New York City's growing Harlem neighborhood
  • 1917: Establishment of the National Beauty Culturists and Benevolent Association of Madam C. J. Walker Agents
  • 1919: Dies from kidney failure and complications of hypertension at the age of 51

“I am a woman who came from the cotton fields of the South. From there I was promoted to the washtub. From there I was promoted to the cook kitchen. And from there I promoted myself into the business of manufacturing hair goods and preparations….I have built my own factory on my own ground.”

-- Madam C.J. Walker, 1912
